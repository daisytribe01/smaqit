# Phases

Phases describe the rhythm of moving from intent to running, validated outcomes. Each phase intertwines specification and implementation to deliver feedback early and keep progress legible.

## Overview

The framework moves through three linked moments: developing a working expression of intent, deploying it into its environment, and validating it against expectations. Each moment finishes before the next begins, making progress reversible and transparent.

## Lifecycle Clarity

When an actor touches a specification, the specification's state reflects that interaction. Status cascades capture reality so lineage stays honest and reviewers can see what has been implemented, deployed, or validated.

## Phase Definitions

### Develop — Build a Working Application
Translates intent, behavior, and stack choices into a running form in an isolated setting. Emphasizes coherence across layers and verifies that the emerging system matches the described behavior.

### Deploy — Run in Target Environment
Moves the working system into its intended surroundings. Aligns infrastructure expression with prior intent and observes health within that context.

### Validate — Verify Spec Compliance
Examines the deployed system against the expectations captured earlier. Coverage insight, observed outcomes, and reported gaps form the closing feedback loop.

## Phase Transitions

A phase hands off when its own definition of completion is met. Successive phases inherit context and outcomes rather than inventing new intent.

## Handling Uncertainty and Failure

Phases invite early signals when inputs are unclear or results diverge. When progress stalls, transparency and human judgment take precedence over blind iteration.

## Spec Change Adaptation

Changes to intent or behavior ripple downstream. Later phases revisit their work whenever upstream understanding evolves.

## Incremental Development

The framework welcomes iterative growth. New intent is added, refined, implemented, and validated in cycles, keeping progress observable at every stage.
