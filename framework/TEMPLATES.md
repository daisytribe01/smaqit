# Templates

Templates provide structural scaffolding so artifacts remain coherent while content stays expressive. They encode shared expectations about shape and vocabulary across specifications, agents, and prompts.

## Template Types

Specification, agent, and prompt templates capture different perspectives yet share a commitment to clarity, consistency, and traceability.

## Placeholder Convention

Placeholders such as `[PLACEHOLDER]` signal where context-specific detail belongs without constraining expression. Consistent placeholder shapes keep intent legible across templates.

## Specification Templates

Specification templates outline how intent, scope, references, and acceptance thinking are recorded. They favor complete stories over partial fragments and keep traceability visible.

## Agent Templates

Agent templates describe how an actor introduces itself, names its inputs and outputs, cites the framework principles it follows, and declares how it recognizes completion.

## Prompt Templates

Prompt templates collect user intent in free-form language with gentle structure for recall and reproducibility. Examples live as comments to inspire without contaminating requirements.

## Template Completeness

A template feels complete when its shape is unambiguous, its placeholders are clear, and its sections invite the right kind of information without prescribing implementation mechanics.
